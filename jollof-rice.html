Classic Nigerian Jollof Rice

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 5 large tomatoes (blended)
  • 2 red bell peppers (blended)
  • 1 large onion (half blended, half sliced)
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (blended)
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 2 tsp curry powder
  • 2 tsp thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and seasoning cubes (to taste)

Instructions

  1. Wash rice thoroughly and parboil for 10 minutes, then rinse and set aside.
  2. Blend tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnet, and half the onion into a smooth paste.
  3. Heat oil in a large pot, add sliced onions and fry until fragrant.
  4. Add tomato paste and fry for 3‚Äì5 minutes to reduce tanginess.
  5. Pour in the blended mix, season with curry, thyme, bay leaves, and salt. Cook until sauce thickens and oil floats on top.
  6. Add chicken stock and stir well.
  7. Add the rice, stir to coat, then reduce the heat to low. Cover tightly and let it steam.
  8. Stir occasionally to prevent burning. Cook until the rice is soft and fluffy.
  9. Remove from heat, fluff with a fork, and serve hot with fried plantain, chicken, or salad.

  • Cook on low heat to achieve the signature smoky flavor.

  • Use a heavy-bottomed pot to avoid burning.
